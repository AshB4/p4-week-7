AWS EC2 Instance Selection

1. Naming Convention Breakdown

Example Instance: m5.2xlarge

m:
Represents the instance family. The "m" family is General Purpose and provides a balanced mix of compute, memory, and networking resources.

5:
Represents the generation of the instance. The number 5 means it is the fifth generation of the M family with newer hardware and better performance improvements.

2xlarge:
Represents the size of the instance. Larger sizes provide additional vCPUs, memory, and networking capacity.

--------------------------------------------------

2. Workload Mapping Case Studies

A. Startup Web Server
Recommended Category:
General Purpose

Recommended Family:
T family (t2.micro under AWS Free Tier)

Explanation:
General Purpose instances provide balanced compute, memory, and networking resources, making them ideal for small web servers, student projects, and startup applications. The t2.micro instance is commonly available in the AWS Free Tier for learning and development purposes.

--------------------------------------------------

B. High-Speed BI Reporting Engine
Recommended Category:
Memory Optimized

Recommended Family:
R family

Explanation:
Memory Optimized instances are designed for workloads requiring high-speed processing of large datasets in memory. Business Intelligence systems benefit from high RAM capacity because they perform large imports, real-time analytics, and database manipulation more efficiently when data is cached in memory.

--------------------------------------------------

C. High-Concurrency Ticketing Platform
Recommended Category:
Storage Optimized

Recommended Family:
I family

Explanation:
Storage Optimized instances provide high disk throughput and high IOPS performance. These instances are ideal for transactional systems such as ticketing platforms that experience massive spikes in simultaneous requests and require fast read/write operations to prevent crashes and bottlenecks.
